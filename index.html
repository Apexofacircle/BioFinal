Creating a resistant bacteria and putting it to the test

The Goal:

The goal is to create a bacteria that is resistant to antibiotics

How:

The bacterial resistance is going to be developed with what I like to call the Princess Bride Method.


The antibiotic is going to be introduced to the bacteria in increments to allow the bacteria to gain a resistance to the antibiotic

Gathering the bacteria

The bacteria in this video is taken from keyboards and a door handle. The bacteria is swabbed into a petri dish made of agar.

Growing the bacteria

After growing the bacteria, I separated the yellow bacteria because I believed it was the same bacteria.


I was able to separate the mold from the bacteria. I used "AMOX SK 1" as the petri dish of choice.


"AMOX" -- Amoxicillin.
"SK" -- Sol's keyboard.
"1" -- First petri dish with the bacteria separated out.


Later in the process of growing and harvesting the bacteria, I introduced it to multiple antibiotics at once to vary its resistant abilities.


The control petri dish had mold growing on it.


After roughly 24 hours, the bacteria was able to overcome the antibiotics. You can see it by the ring around the antibiotic and how the ring was filled in by bacteria.


During this project, I figured out that to kill a bacteria, you have to kill all of it; otherwise, it will grow back from the remaining bacteria.


There is evidence of this in the following image. The rings around the antibiotics are where the bacteria were killed and grew back once it gained resistance to the antibiotic.


The best method I found to kill bacteria is rubbing alcohol. It's what I used to clean my tools, and it is good enough to clean surfaces and hands.


A sample of bacteria mixed with sugar and water turned red over the span of a couple of days. I replicated it on a larger scale to the best of my abilities. I did not take measurements for the original sample. I was more curious if bacteria would survive in sugar water. Note for next time: Take measurements.

The sugar does have contaminants because it was being used to make rocket fuel and was left out in the open for months.


I transferred over the bacteria from the smaller vial to the new larger one to help stem the bacteria growth.

Vocabulary

Penicillin - The first modern antibacterial

Bacteria - Microscopic single-celled organisms

Antibacterial - A type of medication that kills bacteria

Bacterial density - The amount of bacteria in a given space

Agar - The growth medium/food for the bacteria
